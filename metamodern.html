Metamodern Media


*Note: This project was designed for a specific device resolution


Metamodern Media was the final assignment for my Fall 2023 Projects in Digital Composition class. We were tasked with creating an interactive kiosk display based on our chosen topic. Since the projects would be put on public display, they were meant to be as accessible and user-friendly as possible.

Time

2 weeks

Tools

HTML/CSS/JavaScript, Chrome Kiosk App, Figma

Deliverable

Interactive Kiosk Display

01. Ideation + Prototype

Since I had to split my time between design and development, I wanted to make the initial designs very concise. My topic, "metamodernism", describes an emerging cultural philosophy - in other words, it can be pretty dense and difficult to understand. Most of my ideation was spent trying to distill it into a coherent format.

Finding the Form

Showcasing media felt like the most straightforward way to explain metamodernism. Paired with a historical background/overview, the average person could gain an understanding without being overwhelmed. I really liked the idea of different modal windows for different media categories: it would reduce text on-screen, and keep the main screens mostly visual. My main source of inspiration came from Caleb Sun's Guang Yu Kiosk Experience . I really loved the elegant method of displaying various artifacts, and how it's oriented for a touch-based experience.

After bouncing around different ideas, my next step was to coalesce them into a prototype.

I showed this prototype to my class and instructor for feedback. They gave me the following notes:

  • The experience was easy to follow, but didn't feel unique enough

  • Modal windows were a good idea and should be further expanded on

  • Given the "out there" subject matter, the experience should also be breaking some boundaries: I should do more to match function with meaning

02. Creating Unique Interactions

I addressed my feedback by creating unique interactions for each media type. Books emulated flash cards, movies emulated a video player, and art had tooltips. Though this approach definitely took more effort and the majority of my time, it elevated the project and led to people expressing audible delight when interacting with it.

Outcome

Metamodern Media recieved the following:

  • Chosen as the first project for a week-long showcase

  • Article written in The Pitt News

  • Selected to be displayed during prospective students' tours

Overall, I'm happy with how the project turned out in the amount of time I had. If I could work on it again, I would definitely make it responsive + add more content . There was material I cut due to the deadline, but designing for a kiosk was an interesting format that I want to return to.
